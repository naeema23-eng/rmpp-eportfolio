Reflective Activity 1: Ethics in Computing

Unit 1 · Ethics in Computing in the Age of Generative AI

Ethics in Computing in the Age of Generative AI

What – Context and focus

Since late 2022, generative AI (GenAI) tools have rapidly transitioned from research settings into everyday use. They now generate code, text, images and summaries at scale. As Correa et al. (2023) highlight, this expansion has been accompanied by the publication of more than 200 AI ethics guidelines worldwide, differing widely in scope, terminology and enforceability.

So what – Analysis

This fragmentation highlighted the risk of ethics becoming symbolic rather than operational. Professional codes such as the ACM and BCS frameworks emphasise public interest, accountability and competence, which helped me contextualise ethical responsibility beyond technical accuracy.

Now what – Action

This reflection reshaped my understanding of responsible AI practice, especially in healthcare contexts where misuse of data or automation bias can cause direct harm.

Final 1,000-word Reflective Review (Unit 12)

End-of-module critical reflection

What – Overview of learning across the module

This module provided a structured foundation in research methods, ethics, statistics and professional practice within computing. At the beginning of the module, my understanding of research was largely technical and outcome-focused. Through progressive engagement with discussions, research planning activities, statistical exercises and reflective tasks, I developed a more critical and holistic view of how computing research must balance methodological rigour, ethical responsibility and real-world feasibility.

Key artefacts such as the literature review, research proposal, statistical worksheets and reflective activities formed a coherent learning narrative. Rather than isolated tasks, each unit built on previous learning, culminating in an integrated understanding of academic investigation and professional conduct.

So what – Critical reflection and analysis

One of the most significant learning shifts occurred in relation to research design. Early planning activities revealed my tendency to define broad, descriptive topics. Peer review exercises and proposal feedback demonstrated that strong research must align research questions, data availability, methodology and ethical constraints. This insight directly influenced how I refined my proposal scope and justified methodological decisions.

Statistical learning in Units 6–9 challenged my assumptions about data interpretation. I learned that correctness of calculation is insufficient without careful consideration of validity, reliability and presentation. The American Statistical Association’s ethical guidelines reinforced that misleading visualisation or selective reporting can be as harmful as computational errors (American Statistical Association, 2022).

Ethical discussions were particularly impactful given my interest in AI for healthcare. The ACM (2018) and BCS (2021) codes of conduct helped me frame ethics as an ongoing professional obligation rather than a compliance checklist. This perspective was reinforced by case studies involving data misuse, automation bias and survey manipulation, which highlighted the societal consequences of irresponsible computing practices.

Reflecting using Rolfe, Freshwater and Jasper’s (2001) framework enabled deeper self-analysis. I recognised how my initial confidence sometimes masked gaps in methodological reasoning, and how structured reflection supported more deliberate learning and professional growth.

Now what – Application and future development

The learning from this module will directly inform my MSc project and professional practice. Methodologically, I will prioritise feasibility, transparency and justification in research design. Statistically, I will ensure that analysis and visualisation choices communicate uncertainty honestly and ethically. Professionally, I will continue to align my work with established ethical standards and reflect critically on the broader impact of AI systems.

The professional skills matrix and action plan developed in Unit 11 provide a practical roadmap for continuous development, including strengthening advanced statistical skills and pursuing relevant certifications. Most importantly, this module reinforced that responsible computing requires reflection, humility and accountability alongside technical expertise.
