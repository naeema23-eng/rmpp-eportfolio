Reflective Activity 1: Ethics in Computing

Unit 1 · Ethics in Computing in the Age of Generative AI

Reflective Activity 1 asked me to engage critically with the ethical challenges posed by generative AI and to relate these to professional codes such as the ACM and BCS. Drawing on academic literature and my own background in biomedical engineering and AI, I examined how fragmented global AI guidelines, data governance issues and human-centred design all shape responsible GenAI use.

In the reflection, I argued for a risk-tiered, enforceable governance framework that combines global principles, local law, professional codes and concrete engineering practices. I emphasised that ethical practice for computing professionals now includes understanding GenAI limitations, handling sensitive health data responsibly, and designing systems around human physiological and cognitive limits.

This activity established a foundation for the rest of the module: it connected research methods with real-world ethical decision-making, especially in AI-enabled healthcare contexts. The full text of this reflection is presented on the Reflections page.
