About Me

Professional background · Academic direction

My name is Naeema Abdalla Ahmed Alnaqbi, a biomedical engineer working in the UAE with a strong and growing interest in applying Artificial Intelligence responsibly within healthcare environments. Through my work in emergency medical services and medical device management, I have developed an appreciation for how research, evidence and professional ethics shape safe and effective healthcare practice.

I joined the MSc Artificial Intelligence to build the analytical and scientific foundations necessary to integrate AI with biomedical engineering. The module Research Methods and Professional Practice plays a central role in this development because it teaches not only how to conduct research, but also how to do so ethically, rigorously and with professional accountability.

Throughout this module, I have strengthened my understanding of research design, ethical decision-making, academic communication, data integrity and professional codes of conduct. These competencies are essential for my long-term goal of contributing to AI-enabled healthcare systems that emphasise patient safety, fairness, transparency and trust.

This e-portfolio documents my learning journey across all module activities, including collaborative discussions, research planning, literature review development, statistics worksheets and structured reflection. Each component demonstrates how this module has shaped my professional identity and enhanced my confidence as a researcher and AI practitioner.

Completion & Submission

End of Module Assignment status

This e-Portfolio has been completed in accordance with the End of Module Assignment – Individual e-Portfolio requirements for the module Research Methods and Professional Practice. It collates all required artefacts produced throughout the module and provides a structured, evidence-based record of learning.

The portfolio includes:

  • All completed e-Portfolio and reflective activities across Units 1–12
  • Mandatory statistics exercises and worksheets from Units 8 and 9
  • An evaluation of the Literature Review and Research Proposal submissions
  • A final 1,000-word critical reflective review (Unit 12)

The overall written content complies with the 2,500-word total limit, with the final reflective piece clearly identified and capped at approximately 1,000 words. References, figures, screenshots, and supplementary artefacts are excluded from the word count, in line with the assessment guidance.

Submission components:

  • Portfolio website: https://naeema23-eng.github.io/rmpp-eportfolio/
  • Final reflection (PDF / Word) submitted separately via the submission portal, including the portfolio URL in the document header.
