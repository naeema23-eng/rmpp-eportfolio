Unit 8 – Data Analysis and Visualisation

Unit 8 · Charts, distributions, and ethical data presentation

Overview

Unit 8 focused on analysing and presenting quantitative data using appropriate visualisation techniques. Frequency charts, percentage charts, and histograms were used to identify patterns and communicate results clearly and ethically.

Interpretation of Frequency and Percentage Charts

Exercise 8.1D – Single-area analysis

Frequency and percentage charts were used to summarise category distributions (A, B and Other). Percentages allowed proportional comparison, while frequencies showed absolute counts.

Exercises 8.2D and 8.2E – Comparative analysis

Clustered bar charts enabled comparison across areas and locations, revealing contextual differences that could not be identified through aggregated data alone.

Evidence: Unit 8 Charts and Visual Outputs

Ethical Considerations

This unit emphasised the ethical responsibility of analysts to avoid misleading visualisations. Choices related to scaling, categorisation, and presentation directly influence interpretation and must be handled transparently.

Reflection (What? So What? Now What?)

What?

I learned how to construct and interpret frequency charts, percentage charts, and histograms.

So what?

Effective visualisation supports accurate interpretation and evidence-based decision-making.

Now what?

I will apply these principles in my MSc project and professional practice.

Learning Outcomes Addressed

  • Evaluation of data analysis processes
  • Critical interpretation of quantitative results
  • Ethical and professional judgement in data presentation
