Unit 6 – Descriptive Statistics and Levels of Measurement

Unit 6 · Understanding quantitative methods

Unit 6 introduced quantitative methods as a way of examining relationships between variables that are measured numerically and organised into datasets. A dataset can be thought of as a table where each row represents an observation and each column represents a variable of interest.

The focus of this unit was on descriptive statistics. Measures of location (mean and median) describe a “typical” observation, while measures of spread (range, variance and standard deviation) provide insight into data variability.

In the context of predicting patient deterioration in ambulance settings, descriptive statistics are essential for understanding baseline physiological patterns before applying inferential or predictive models.

Unit 8 – Data Analysis and Visualisation

Unit 8 · Charts, distributions, and ethical data presentation

Overview

Unit 8 focused on analysing and presenting quantitative data using appropriate visualisation techniques. Through structured Excel exercises, I explored frequency distributions, percentage comparisons and histograms to identify patterns and communicate results clearly and ethically.

Interpretation of Frequency and Percentage Charts

Exercise 8.1D – Single-area analysis

Frequency and percentage charts were used to summarise category distributions (A, B and Other). Percentages provided proportional insight, while frequencies showed absolute scale.

Exercises 8.2D and 8.2E – Comparative analysis

Clustered bar charts enabled comparison between areas and locations, revealing contextual differences that would be obscured by aggregated totals alone.

Evidence: Unit 8 Charts and Visual Outputs

Ethical Considerations

This unit highlighted the ethical responsibility of analysts to avoid misleading visualisations. Chart selection, scaling and category inclusion directly influence interpretation and must be handled transparently to maintain professional integrity.

Reflection (What? So What? Now What?)

What?

I learned to construct and interpret frequency charts, percentage charts and histograms for quantitative datasets.

So what?

Visualisation choices shape understanding. Poor design can mislead, while clear charts support evidence-based decision-making.

Now what?

I will apply these principles in my MSc project and professional work, ensuring transparent, ethical presentation of analytical results.

Learning Outcomes Addressed

  • Evaluation of data analysis processes
  • Critical interpretation of quantitative results
  • Ethical and professional judgement in data presentation
