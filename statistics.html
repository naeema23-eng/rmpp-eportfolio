Statistics Worksheets (Units 7–10)

Units 7–10 · Statistical analysis and interpretation

Later in the module, Units 7–10 will focus on statistical concepts that support sound research design. This page is ready to store the completed worksheets and a short commentary once those activities are done.

Planned coverage

  • Descriptive statistics (mean, median, standard deviation, ranges)
  • Basic inferential ideas (significance, p-values, simple tests)
  • Working with variables and relationships between them
  • Interpreting outputs in relation to research questions

After I complete each worksheet, I will add a concise summary here that explains:

  • Which dataset was used
  • Which statistical techniques were applied
  • What the main findings were and how they relate to research methods

Charts Example Worksheet (Unit 9)

Unit 9 · Visualising data and communicating results

The Unit 9 charts example will emphasise choosing appropriate visualisations and explaining results to non-specialist audiences. This section is reserved for that activity.

When the Unit 9 worksheet is complete, I will use this space to describe:

  • Which chart types I used (for example, bar charts, boxplots, histograms)
  • Why these were suitable for the variables and research question
  • How I would explain the charts and results in clear, simple language
